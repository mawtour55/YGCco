    Nami Island: Nami Island is famous for the Korean Drama “Winter Sonata” shooting place and its beautiful tree lined roads

    Petite France: The concept of Petite France encapsulates ‘flowers, stars, and the Little Prince.’ Also, this place is famous for the Korean entertainment shows “Running Man” shooting place

    Gyeongbok Palace: Constructed in 1395, this was the first palace of the Joseon Dynasty. It is the grandest of the 5 palaces in Seoul and portrays the essence of the Korean royal court culture.

    Bukchon Hanok Village: This traditional village is composed of lots of alleys, Hanok and is preserved to show a 600-year-old urban environment.

    Insadong: located in the heart of the city, is an important place where old but precious and traditional goods are on display. Within these alleys are galleries and traditional restaurants, teahouses, and cafes.

    Namsan Park: You will take the cable car up to the top of Namsan Park for a magnificent view of Seoul.

    Everland: A large theme park with over 40 heart pounding rides and attractions, boasting a Safari Would, snow sled (winter season only) and botanical garden, it is one of the most popular places in Korea to have fun.

    Pocheon Art Valley: Oddly shaped rocks and a beautiful lake were once a closed quarry. The transformation was the outcome of Korea's first environment restoration project led by Pocheon-si. As of now, more than 1 million tourists visit this place every year.

    Hantan river Sky bridge: Also known for the filming location for Crash Landing on You, the Hantan river Sky bridge is 200 meters long and allows 1,500 adults (80kg) to pass at the same time, and offers a 50m view of the Hantan River Canyon. You will find a glass floor in the middle of the Sky bridge. Let's take a deep breath and cross the bridge!

    Bidulginang Waterfalls: In the old times, pigeons, or bidulgi in Korean, lived inside the cave behind the waterfall over a hollow cliff that resembled a pigeon’s nest, which is how the name “Bidulginang Waterfalls” originated. The waterfall is surrounded by grand columnar joints. The landscape appears quite mysterious and serene, earning it a spot as a popular filming site for historical dramas. It was also a shooting place for Netflix drama Kingdom.

    Pocheon Herb Island: It is the largest herbal botanical garden in Korea. The attractions are endless, from the lovely pink wish tunnel to Santa’s Village, featuring Santa House and Santa Church. The photo spots throughout the park are why it is popular among couples.

    Hyupjae & Gumneung Beach Hyupjae Beach is one of the popular places that many tourists visit in anytime. The white sand beach has extra fine sand mixed with shells that glitters with silver, and the emerald-colored water is crystal clear.

    O’sulloc Green tea field: O'sulloc Museum teaches visitors about Korea's traditional tea culture. The “o“ in “o'sulloc“, means to appreciate and enjoy, and it also stands for the origin of sulloc, only sulloc, and of sulloc tea.

    Teddy Bear Museum: Teddy Bear Museum offers a lot of interesting things to see with various themes such as history and art of Seoul and a tour around the world. Displayed Teddy bears are handmade by designers with premium goat wool for high quality.

    Jusangjeoli Hexagon Lava Cliff [UNESCO GEO Park]: They look like a large folding screen with black hexagon stone pillars. When you visit here and look at the columnar joints with beautiful ocean waves crashing against them, you will be mesmerized by nature's greatness.

    Cheonjeyeon Waterfall: This 3-level waterfall is located amid a warm temperate forest. The rock face that resembles columnar joints and the emerald-colored pond make for a beautiful landscape.

    Mysterious Road: It is called the Jeju Mysterious Road or Dokkaebi Road. It earned its name because when you place a car on this steep road, the car does not roll down, but it goes up, defying gravity. This is an optical illusion, in which the surroundings trick the eye and make the road look like it is tilted in the opposite direction.

    Kimnyeong Beach: The Kimnyeong Beach offers a beautiful view, blending soft sand and clear blue sea water on a small white sandy beach

    Seongeup Folk Village: Seongeup Folk Village is a small town that holds a vast amount of culture. Cultural properties in the folk village have been handed down from generation to generation and include treasures such as residential houses, Confucian shrines and schools, ancient government offices, stone statues, large millstones (pulled by horses or ox), fortress ruins, and stone monuments.

    Seongsan Ilchulbong Peak [UNESCO World Heritage] (Trekking : 60 minutes): Seongsan Ilchulbong Peak rose from under the sea in a volcanic eruption over 100,000 years ago. There is a huge crater at the top of Seongsan Ilchulbong Peak. The crater is about 600m in diameter and 90m high. With the 99 sharp rocks surrounding the crater, it looks like a gigantic crown.

    Via Jongdalri Shore Road: The tour car takes beautiful coastline of North-Eastern Jeju and you will pass by Hado Beach & Saehwa beach. Jongdalri Shore Road is also known as for a hydrangea during Summer.
